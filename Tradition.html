Maranao
The Maranao is, in fact, among the biggest Islamic communities in the Philippines alongside other tribes of the Bangsamoro such as the Maguindanaon and tausug. The Original endonym of the ancestral Maranao is believed to be "Iranaon" that soon became Maguindanao and the Iranun people.
MARANAO TRADITION
The cultural traditions of the Maranao people encompass various aspects such as the utilization of the Maranao language, adherence to traditional birthing customs, and the engagement in 'rido', an enduring cultural phenomenon characterized by inter-family feuds and conflicts.
Maranao Arts
Maranao arts, originating from the Maranao people of the Philippines, are highly significant culturally and artistically. They are known for intricate craftsmanship, particularly in textiles (malong), brassware (gongs and kulintang ensembles), and woodcarving (torogan architecture). These arts serve as expressions of identity, cultural heritage, and communal values among the Maranao people, embodying their rich traditions and societal roles.
Maranao Cuisine
Maranao cuisine is celebrated for its rich variety of tastes, textures, and aromas, blending traditional dishes with innovative cooking methods. "Palapa" and "Tiula Itum" are popular dishes made with key ingredients like rice, fish, coconut, and flavorful spices. Cooking techniques such as grilling and steaming enhance the natural flavors of locally sourced ingredients, while traditional practices like "Inulukan" add a unique smoky aroma to dishes.
Maranao Clothing
Maranao women are known for dressing themselves in apparel that carries a cultural significance that is distinct to their community. The malong, among other types of clothing worn by them, is an adaptable fabric whose various uses make it outstanding. It is used mainly by females as either dress, skirt, blouse or even wedding dress though it can be more than this personal wear aspect. Within the family context, the malong goes beyond what is normally expected of it.
